## What Is a Thyroid Test?

The thyroid is a small butterfly-shaped gland in your neck that produces hormones regulating metabolism, energy levels, heart rate, and body temperature. When it produces too much or too little hormone, it affects nearly every system in the body.

A thyroid test measures the levels of key hormones in your blood:

| Test | What It Measures | Normal Range |
|---|---|---|
| **TSH** (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) | Pituitary signal to the thyroid — most sensitive indicator | 0.4–4.0 mIU/L |
| **T3** (Triiodothyronine) | Active thyroid hormone | 100–200 ng/dL |
| **T4** (Thyroxine) | Stored thyroid hormone | 5.0–12.0 µg/dL |
| **Free T3 / Free T4** | Unbound (active) fractions — more accurate | See lab reference |
| **Anti-TPO Antibodies** | Detects autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto's) | <35 IU/mL |

Most doctors start with **TSH alone**, then order T3/T4 if the TSH is outside the normal range.

## When Should You Get a Thyroid Test?

You should consider a thyroid test if you experience:

**Signs of Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid):**
- Persistent fatigue and sluggishness
- Unexplained weight gain
- Feeling cold all the time
- Dry skin, brittle nails, hair loss
- Depression or brain fog
- Constipation
- Irregular or heavy periods

**Signs of Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid):**
- Unexplained weight loss
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat
- Anxiety or restlessness
- Excessive sweating
- Tremors in hands
- Frequent bowel movements

**Routine screening is also recommended for:**
- Women over 30 (thyroid disorders are 5–10x more common in women)
- Anyone with a family history of thyroid disease
- Pregnant women or those trying to conceive
- People with type 1 diabetes or other autoimmune conditions
- Patients on lithium or amiodarone medication

## Do You Need to Fast Before a Thyroid Test?

No. **Thyroid tests do not require fasting.** You can eat and drink normally before giving your sample. However, if your thyroid test is being done alongside other tests that require fasting (such as blood sugar or lipid profile), you should follow the fasting instructions.

## Understanding Your Thyroid Test Results

Your TSH result is the most important number:

- **TSH below 0.4 mIU/L** — suggests hyperthyroidism (overactive)
- **TSH between 0.4–4.0 mIU/L** — normal range
- **TSH above 4.0 mIU/L** — suggests hypothyroidism (underactive)
- **TSH above 10 mIU/L** — overt hypothyroidism; treatment typically recommended

Your doctor will interpret these numbers in the context of your symptoms, age, and any medications you are taking. Do not self-diagnose based on numbers alone.
