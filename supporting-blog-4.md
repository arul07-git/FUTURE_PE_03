## Do You Need to Fast Before a Blood Test?

Not all blood tests require fasting. It depends on what is being measured.

### Tests That REQUIRE Fasting (8–12 hours)

| Test | Fast Duration |
|---|---|
| Fasting Blood Glucose | 8–12 hours |
| Lipid Profile (Cholesterol) | 10–12 hours |
| HbA1c (usually no fast needed, confirm with doctor) | Optional |
| Liver Function Test (LFT) | 8–10 hours |
| Kidney Function Test (KFT) | 8 hours |
| Iron Studies | 8–12 hours |
| Full Body Checkup Packages | 10–12 hours |

### Tests That Do NOT Require Fasting

| Test | Can Eat Normally? |
|---|---|
| Complete Blood Count (CBC) | Yes |
| Thyroid Profile (TSH, T3, T4) | Yes |
| Vitamin D / B12 | Yes |
| HbA1c | Usually yes — confirm with doctor |
| Pregnancy test (blood) | Yes |
| Covid antibody test | Yes |

> **Thyroid tests specifically:** No fasting required. [Learn more about thyroid test costs in Chennai](supporting-blog-3.md).

**When in doubt, fast.** If your test order is unclear or combines fasting and non-fasting tests, it is always safer to fast for 10 hours and do all tests together in one blood draw.

## What Can You Have During the Fasting Period?

### You CAN Have:
- **Plain water** — drink it freely; being hydrated actually makes veins easier to find
- **Plain black coffee or tea** (no sugar, no milk) — acceptable for some tests, but check with your doctor to be certain

### You CANNOT Have:
- Juice, soft drinks, milk, flavoured water
- Food of any kind — even a small biscuit or fruit
- Sweetened or milky tea/coffee
- Chewing gum or mints
- Alcohol (avoid for at least 24 hours before a lipid profile)

---

## Medication: What to Do

**Do NOT stop any prescription medication without speaking to your doctor first.** Most routine medications (blood pressure tablets, thyroid medication, etc.) can be taken normally on the morning of your test.

However, some situations require specific timing:
- **Biotin (Vitamin B7) supplements** can interfere with several hormone tests — stop at least 72 hours before if possible
- **Iron supplements** should ideally be stopped 24 hours before an iron panel
- **Thyroid medication (Levothyroxine)** — take it after your blood draw, not before, if your doctor wants to check your fasting TSH level. Ask your doctor.

## Morning of Your Blood Test — Practical Tips

1. **Schedule early morning appointments** — 7–9 AM is ideal. You sleep through most of your fast, and you can eat normally straight after.
2. **Drink two glasses of water before you leave** — proper hydration makes veins more visible and blood draws faster and less painful.
3. **Wear a short-sleeved or loose-sleeved top** — rolling up a tight sleeve is uncomfortable for everyone.
4. **Bring your test order and ID** — your doctor's prescription, and any prior reports if this is a follow-up test.
5. **Avoid vigorous exercise the morning of the test** — intense exercise can temporarily alter white blood cell counts, creatinine, and potassium levels.

## After Your Blood Test

- **Eat something as soon as you can** — especially if you have been fasting. Keep a snack ready.
- **Press the cotton swab firmly** for 2–3 minutes after the draw to prevent bruising.
- **Mild soreness** at the puncture site is normal and fades within a day. A cold pack helps if the area feels tender.
